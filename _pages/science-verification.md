Science Verification (SV) is run by the SKAO, with a limited number of SV projects supported on SRCNet resources. The UKSRC will extend this by supporting **additional UK-led SV projects on our national resources, outside of the SRCNet allocation** — so that more UK-affiliated teams can carry out verification work even where SRCNet capacity is full.

## What is science verification?

Science Verification tests specific observing modes and capabilities of the telescopes — for example integration times, numbers of beams, and other instrument configurations — ahead of full science operations. SKAO plans for SV targets and Observatory-led data products will feed into this process.

## The SKAO science verification process

SV itself is run by the SKAO and is expected to follow roughly this timeline ahead of observations:

1. **Call for ideas (~6 months before)** — a call issued to the community for verification ideas that test specific modes and capabilities. Expected to be open for around two months.
2. **Idea submission (~6–4 months before)** — submit a verification idea through the SV idea submission tool, including a title, the mode/capability to be tested, a summary of the idea and verification opportunities, and the target.
3. **Idea assessment (~4 months before)** — a light-touch internal appraisal producing a pool of well-suited, prioritised targets and ideas. Breadth of science coverage will be considered to ensure diverse user engagement.
4. **Announcement of SV target set (~3 months before)** — SV targets are announced, including Observatory-led data product plans.
5. **Call for SRCNet resource requests (~2.5 months before)** — resource availability begins, building as we move through the SV stages. Requests will include a verification plan for the SV target, the intention to provide feedback, and feasibility.

## How UKSRC support will work

Alongside the SKAO process above, the UKSRC will run a **coincident call for resources on our national facilities**, timed to open with the SRCNet resource requests (step 5). Because SRCNet can only support a limited number of SV projects, this call lets us take on **additional UK-led projects on national resources** — giving UK teams a route to do their verification work even when SRCNet capacity is full.

- **National resource allocation** — UKSRC compute for UK-led SV projects that sit outside the SRCNet allocation.
- **Advanced HPC training** to support teams in carrying out their verification work.

Successful applicants will be expected to provide feedback and a verification plan for their SV target, mirroring the SRCNet process.

## Who can apply

Participation in the UKSRC coincident call is **restricted to PIs with UK affiliations**.
