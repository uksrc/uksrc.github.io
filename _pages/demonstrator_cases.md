The ukSRC Demonstrator Case Programme is a central mechanism for co-developing the UK SRC Node in partnership with UK science users. Identified through open community calls, town halls, and workshops, the initial cases are led by researchers from across UK universities and represent a broad cross-section of SKA science priorities.

These demonstrator cases are designed to stress-test SRC capabilities as they are developed, ensuring that data systems, tools, and workflows meet real scientific needs. By focusing on leading pathfinder projects, the programme both pushes technical limits and captures common user requirements that can be generalised across the community.

A continuous development model underpins the programme: cases are reviewed and updated annually, with new cases added as science priorities evolve. This ensures ongoing community engagement and aligns SRC development with emerging research directions and technologies.

The approach is mutually beneficial. The SRC provides resources and infrastructure to support demonstrator science, while the community uses these resources to actively shape and validate the system. This co-design and co-development cycle strengthens UK leadership in SKA science, builds user skills and expertise, and ensures that the SRC evolves as a usable, adoptable platform.

Through this process, the programme not only advances technical development but also supports scientific discovery, community growth, and stronger links between SRCNet and the UK research community.
